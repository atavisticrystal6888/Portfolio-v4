## Context & Problem

The marketing team was running campaigns across multiple channels — social media, email, paid search, content — but had no unified way to compare effectiveness. Budget was allocated based on intuition and historical precedent, not performance data.

The core question: "Which channels are actually driving customers, and where should we shift budget?"

## Research & Discovery

- Audited campaign data across 4 channels: paid social, email, search ads, content marketing
- Identified key metrics gap: no consistent attribution model across channels
- Mapped the conversion funnel per channel to understand where drop-offs happen

## Solution & Approach

### 1. Data Unification

Consolidated campaign data from 4 channels into a single analysis framework with consistent metrics (CPC, CPL, conversion rate, ROI).

### 2. Channel Comparison

Built a standardized scoring model comparing channels on cost efficiency, conversion quality, and customer lifetime value indicators.

### 3. Funnel Analysis

Mapped conversion funnels per channel: impression → click → lead → trial → paid. Identified where each channel's funnel leaked.

### 4. Optimization Dashboard

Power BI dashboards with channel ROI comparison, funnel visualization, and budget allocation recommendations based on performance.

## Outcome & Metrics

- **Channels Analyzed:** 4 — Social, Email, Search, Content
- **Impact:** ROI+ — Data-driven budget reallocation
- **Dashboards:** 2 — ROI Comparison + Funnel View

| Channel | ROI Multiplier | Cost per Lead |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| Paid Social | 2.1x | $28 |
| Email | 3.8x | $12 |
| Search Ads | 1.9x | $35 |
| Content | 4.2x | $8 |

## Learnings

### What Worked

Standardizing metrics across channels was the breakthrough. Once everyone was looking at the same numbers in the same way, the reallocation decisions became obvious.

### What I'd Change

Would add attribution modeling beyond last-touch. Content marketing looked weak on direct conversion but was likely feeding the top of funnel for other channels. Multi-touch attribution would paint a fairer picture.
